OAuth 2.0 and OpenID Connect (OIDC) exist to solve a simple problem: enabling one application to securely access data from another.

The logic is straightforward, but the overlapping terminology obscures the mechanics.

This guide translates the jargon into a concrete, visual mental model.

The four structural actors in any OAuth architecture

The Resource Owner
The User. You, sitting at your device, deciding what to share.

The Client
The Application. The app that wants to access your data.

The Authorization Server
The Auth Server. The system that verifies your consent and issues tokens.

The Resource Server
The API. The system holding your secure data, waiting for a valid token.
